Katsu Curry

Where Did I Try Katsu Curry for the First Time?

When I was traveling through Japan, I made the city of Osaka my jumping-off point for other nearby towns. For this dish, I made my way to Nara. Nara was home to the beautiful, grand temple complex called Todai-Ji. After exploring the temple and the surrounding areas, I headed towards a restaurant famously known for having one of the best Katsu Curry dishes in Nara. I decided to omit the shrimp that usually comes with this dish because, at the time, I wasn’t sure how big the serving was going to be. It was one of my favorite meals I had the pleasure to eat during my trip to Japan!

Ingredients

    400g / 0.9lb onion sliced into 1cm / 3/8” wide piece
    250g / 0.6lb potato cut into 1.5cm / 5/8” cubes
    100g / 3.5oz carrot sliced to 7mm / 1/4” thick pieces (note 1)
    1 tbsp oil
    1/2 packet of 230g / 0.5lb House Vermont Curry (Mild, note 2)
    800ml / 1.7pt water
    4 cups cooked rice (hot)
    4 Chicken Cutlets cut into 2.5cm / 1” wide strips (note 3)
    Fukujinzuke as a condiment (if you have it)

Steps

  • Put oil into a pot and sauté onion pieces until the edges start browning
  • Add potato and carrot pieces to the pot and stir.
  • Add water and bring it to a boil. Remove scum as it rises.
  • Add curry roux and cook.
  • Cook chicken cutlets and slice them.
  • Put cooked rice on one side of a serving plate, place cutlet pieces next to the rice.
  • Pour curry on the side next to the cutlet away from the rice.
  • Place a small amount of fukujinzuke on the plate.
