Matcha Waffles

Where Did I Try Matcha Waffles?

Before ordering these matcha waffles in Japan, I had never tried matcha except that one occasion when I ordered matcha iced tea at Starbucks. During my travels to Japan, I visited the city of Kyoto, known for its refined culture, dining, and temples. On a whim, I decided to take a detour to the small town of Uji to glimpse the Byodo-In temple. Uji is also known to produce the highest grade of matcha in Japan. After visiting the Byodo-In temple, I walked the town of Uji to locate a restaurant that served a matcha-based brunch. I tracked a small restaurant overlooking the Uji River and ordered Matcha waffles with matcha tea.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour all-purpose
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon matcha powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 3/4 cups almond milk or milk of choice
  • 1/2 cup butter melted (plant based or dairy)
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Preheat your waffle iron according to manufacturer's instructions.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, matcha powder, and salt.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk the eggs and milk.
  4. Pour the milk mixture over the flour mixture and whisk gently. Then pour in the melted butter and gently whisk to combine. Do not overmix.
  5. Add the batter to the preheated waffle iron. (My standard size waffle iron uses about a 1/2 cup batter, whereas my mini waffle maker uses about 3 tablespoons).
  6. Cook waffles until brown and crisp. This will take less than 2 minutes for a mini waffle, and 2-3 minutes for a standard size waffle.
  7. Serve warm, with maple syrup, butter, and/or choice of toppings.
